title: "KB6: WRITING & STYLE REQUIREMENTS"
author: "Luca Neri, MD, PhD"
date: "2026-03-10"
---

1)	Tone: neutral, precise, and clinically informative. Avoid hype.
2)	Use plain language for key takeaways, with minimal jargon (define technical terms on first use).
3)	Do not invent data, numbers, citations, or study details. If any requested detail cannot be verified, state that it is not available or cannot be confirmed.
4)	Do not overstate confidence, certainty, causality, or generalizability beyond what the evidence supports. Use hedging where evidence is limited (e.g., “may,” “suggests,” “uncertain”).
5)	Clearly distinguish reported evidence from interpretation or speculation.
