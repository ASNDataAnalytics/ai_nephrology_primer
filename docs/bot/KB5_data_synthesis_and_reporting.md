## INPUT:
Use tables from Step 4 as appropriate.
## PROCESS:
1) Group and count studies by:
a. Design (e.g., RCTs, prospective cohorts, SR/MAs)
b. Population subgroup
c. Intervention class
d. Outcome domain
2) Summarize the provisional certainty of the body of evidence across key outcome domains, using adapted GRADE domains when appropriate and explicitly labeling this as adapted for narrative synthesis.
3) Highlight influential, methodologically important, or clinically informative studies and the best available syntheses, with brief justification.
4) When quantitative results are comparable, report ranges and typical effect sizes.
5) Explicitly reconcile discordant findings (methodological differences, risk of bias, heterogeneity, dosages, settings).
6) Identify evidence gaps and, when available from retrieved sources, note relevant ongoing trials. 
## DELIVERABLES:
1) Executive summary (≤300 words): 5–8 bullet key points with strengths and limitations.
Always add the following sentence as a disclaimer note at the end of the Executive summary: “This review provides a rapid systematic narrative overview based primarily on title/abstract screening and available retrieved source material; findings should be interpreted as provisional.”
2) Structured Abstract: Background, Methods (narrative approach), Results (themes/effects), Conclusions. 
Always add the following sentence as a disclaimer note at the end of the Structured Abstract: “This review provides a rapid systematic narrative overview based primarily on title/abstract screening and available retrieved source material; findings should be interpreted as provisional.”
3) Main text with headings:
Always add the following sentence as a disclaimer note under the title of the main text: “This systematic narrative review used structured search, title/abstract-based study selection, and extraction from available retrieved source material to provide a rapid overview of the topic. Accordingly, eligibility assessments, extracted details, and evidence appraisals should be interpreted as provisional.”
a. Introduction (problem size, pathophysiology / biologic rationale where relevant, research question)
b. Methods (search, title/abstract-based selection, extraction from available retrieved source material, appraisal—concise but transparent)
c. Evidence overview (organized narrative synthesis as per plan)
d. Discussion (clinical implications, contradictions, limitations of evidence, generalizability)
e. Future directions & research priorities
f. Conclusion (concise, non-speculative)
g. Tables:
* Tables 1, 2, 3 and 4 from Phase 4. 
h. Appendices: 
* Deliverables D1 and D2 from Phase 2 (KB2)

m. References: Vancouver style with DOI/PMID
n. SANRA checklist: brief self-audit table showing how criteria were met
