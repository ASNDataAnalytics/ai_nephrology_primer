DATA SYNTHESIS (NARRATIVE)
INPUT: Use tables from step 4 as appropriate. 
PROCESS: 
1)	Group and count studies by: 
a.	Design (e.g., RCTs, prospective cohorts, SR/MAs) 
b.	Population subgroup 
c.	Intervention class 
d.	Outcome domain
2)	Count papers with high, moderate, low evidence GRADE. 
3)	Highlight landmark studies and best available syntheses.
4)	When quantitative results are comparable, report ranges and typical effect sizes
5)	Explicitly reconcile discordant findings (methodological differences, risk-of-bias, heterogeneity, dosages, settings).
6)	Identify evidence gaps, ongoing trials, and plausible mechanistic explanations (flag when speculative).
DELIVERABLES: 
1)	Executive summary (≤300 words): 5–8 bullet key points with strength/limitations.
2)	Abstract (structured): Background, Methods (narrative approach), Results (themes/effects), Conclusions.
3)	Main text with headings:
a.	Introduction (problem size, pathophysiology/biologic rationale where relevant)
b.	Methods (search, selection, extraction, appraisal—concise but transparent)
c.	Evidence overview (organized narrative synthesis as per plan)
d.	Discussion (clinical implications, contradictions, limitations of evidence, generalizability)
e.	Future directions & research priorities
f.	Conclusion (concise, non-speculative)
g.	Tables/Appendices:
h.	Study characteristics table (one row per study)
i.	Outcome summary table (effect estimates, absolute risk differences where possible)
j.	Risk-of-bias summary (qualitative)
k.	Search strategy log (exact PubMed strings, dates, hits)
l.	List of excluded studies with reasons (brief)
m.	References: Vancouver style with DOI/PMID.
n.	SANRA checklist: brief self-audit table showing how criteria were met.
